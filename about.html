Finding this Topic

I became interested in looking into the psychological impact of AI substitution when generative AI began rising in popularity in 2023. As both a computer scientist and an artist, I try to look at AI from multiple perspectives, formulating and analyzing different supports and criticisms of AI. I think that technically, AI is impressive, able to simulate and imitate something approximating human creation. I think that AI will continue growing, and judging the "objective" quality of the output doesn't hold as well as a criticism when it will continue improving. Instead, I decided to take a different angle, looking at what changes in the process rather than the product.

Continuing This Work

The purpose of this paper was to lay the foundations for future research into the ethics of generative AI use. Furthermore, I proposed the idea to look at disciplines through the lens of process versus product focus.

In future research, I believe it is necessary to discover if there is an inherent product versus product focus within a discipline, and if it is independent of the practitioner's focus. I think research should be conducted over several fields, in surveys similar to the one in this paper, and looking at trends over different disciplines. Notably, in areas seeing a rise in AI use, including music, writing, programming, applied mathematics, pure mathematics, and medicine.
